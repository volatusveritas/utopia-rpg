Characters
Character Creation

Steps

  1. Choose a species or subspecies (either standard or custom) and fill in the values it provides.
  2. Set all of your defenses to 1.
  3. Set your XP to 0 out of 1,000.
  4. Set your Spendable Points to 10. Invest them into talents and choose a specialist talent (you may save up to 5 Spendable Points).
  5. Look into Derived Character Values to fill the remaining values.
Traits and Subtraits

Dependency Tree

  • Body limits your Strength subtraits (Power and Fortitude) and Agility subtraits (Speed and Dexterity).
  • Mind limits your Intellect subtraits (Engineering and Memory) and Will subtraits (Resolve and Awareness).
  • Soul limits your Display subtraits (Portrayal and Stunt) and Charm subtraits (Appeal and Language).

Trait and Subtrait Modifiers

Each trait and subtrait has a modifier that is equal to its value minus four. This is the value used in rolls when they require use of the trait or subtrait.

Gifted Subtraits

If you are gifted in a subtrait, the maximum value for that subtrait is doubled and its modifier can not go below zero.

Agility

Your Agility is the sum of your Speed and your Dexterity.

Speed
How fast you can run in meters per second and how quickly you can gain momentum. Affects how far you can move in a turn.
Dexterity
How diligent your fine motor functions are. Affects how accurate you are with ranged weapons.

Strength

Your Strength is the sum of your Power and your Fortitude.

Power
How much effort you can put into a swift motion. Affects melee attacks and leap distance.
Fortitude
How much your body can handle, be it damage or negative conditions.

Intellect

Your Intellect is the sum of your Engineering and your Memory.

Engineering
How well you operate and manipulate working systems, technological or otherwise. Affects crafting.
Memory
How well you can recall previously processed information.

Will

Your Will is the sum of your Resolve and your Awareness.

Resolve
How you react under pressure how much mental taxation you can withstand. Affects the power of spells you can cast.
Awareness
How alert, observant, and prepared—both passively and actively—you are.

Display

Your Display is the sum of your Portrayal and your Stunt.

Portrayal
Helps you resemble something you're not, replicate social behaviour, intimidate others, and lie.
Stunt
How well you can keep your balance and position yourself regardless of your grounding. Affects how stealth you can be.

Charm

Your Charm is the sum of your Appeal and your Language.

Appeal
How well you can communicate to others.
Language
How eloquently you can speak and express ideas in a language. Affects how quickly you can learn a new language.
Character Advancement

Character Level

Your character's level is the sum of your Spendable Points and your Body, Mind, and Soul scores.

Target Experience

Your target experience is equal to your character's level times a hundred. If you reach or surpass your target experience, you lose that amount of experience and level up.

Level Up

Once you level up:

  • Increase one of your subtraits by one.
  • You get a Spendable Point.
  • If your new level is a multiple of ten, choose a specialist talent.
  • Remember to recalculate Derived Character Values that are modified by your changes.
Derived Character Values

Health and Stamina

  • Your maximum Surface Health Points (SHP) is your Body times your Constitution plus your level.
  • Your maximum Deep Health Points (DHP) is your Soul times your Effervescence plus your level.
  • Your maximum Stamina is your Mind times your Endurance plus your level.

Travel Speeds

  • Your Land travel is equal to your Speed score.
  • Your Water travel is equal to half of your Speed score.
  • Your Air travel is zero.

These values may be modified by talents, items, or other effects.

Rolls and Tests

Test Roll

A test roll is made based on one of the twelve subtraits. To perform a test, roll 3d6 and add the subtrait's modifier.

The test is done against a test difficulty (TD). If the final value of the roll is equal to or higher than the difficulty, the roll is a success. Otherwise, it is a failure.

Contest Rolls

When against another creature or active opposition, your test will be compared to another test instead of a test difficulty. The higher roll will win the contest.

When a tie happens check for these in order:

  1. If it's a PC versus an NPC, the PC wins.
  2. If one is defending themselves, they win.
  3. The one with the higher modifier value wins.
  4. The one with higher level or difficulty rating wins.
  5. One is chosen at random as the winner.

Favor and Disfavor

Situations and conditions or limitations may sway in your favour or against you, in which case you will gain favour or disfavour.

For each point of favour, roll an additional 1d6. For each point of disfavour, roll one less 1d6.

With enough disfavour to roll zero dice, the test is automatically failed.

Critical Successes and Failures

If three or more 6 are rolled, it is considered a critical success. In this case, the roll's value, before modifiers, is doubled. This results in an positive extreme outcome.

If every die rolled is a 1, it is considered a critical failure. The roll's value becomes zero and ignores modifiers. This results in a negative extreme outcome.

Foraging

Foraging Rules

While in a forageable area, decide the target component and amount of time you'll spend foraging. The GM rolls the difficulty based on target component type and rarity. Make a test (usually Awareness). If you succeed, roll amount found (yield). If you fail full time is spent and no resource found.

If you succeed by over a multiple of the difficulty, divide the original search time by it, and that's how much time you spent.

Crafting

Crafting Rules

You need a tool station and one of the talents of the Innovation tree to craft a component. Make an Engineering test and spend a specific amount of components. If you succeed, you create the target component. If you fail, the components used are destroyed.

A tool station is a stationary structure that has the tools and resources necessary to fabricate any item. It should have carving tools, metallurgy kits, versatile adhesives, and basic manufacturing devices as well as kinetic dust or duchess substrate.

The more Rarity Points (RP) an item has, the more valuable and rare it becomes. If an item has negative RP, treat it as positive (absolute of) instead. Items have a minimum RP of 1.

  • Takes up 1 slot.

  • Creatures holding the artifact with 1 hand are considered affected or may choose a taret within 0 meters to be affected.
  • Requires 1 hand to activate.
  • Takes up 1 slot.

Ammunition

  • This artifact is considered ammunition. Targets of attacks using this ammunition are considered affected.
  • Activated features for this item are triggered during each attack upon each successful weapon attack and will cause this item to be consumed and multiply the total feature RP cost by 6.
  • Crafting this artifact yields 4 times as many, making a single item's value a fourth, round down.
  • Rather than each item taking up 1 slot, any number of the item takes up 1 slot.
  • Any amount of the same ammunition may be equipped in the waist or back slot.
